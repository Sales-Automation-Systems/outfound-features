# Copy & Offer Analytics

### Structured A/B Testing at Scale -- Powered by AI

This is one of Outfound's most innovative features. Instead of manually tagging and comparing email variants, Outfound uses AI to automatically analyze the structure of your email copy, tag it across multiple dimensions, and correlate those tags with performance metrics. The result: you don't just know *which* email won -- you know *why*.

---

## How It Works

### Step 1: AI Analyzes Your Copy
When you create email sequences, Outfound's AI engine automatically reads the content and classifies it across 7+ structural dimensions:

- **Subject line style** -- Question, statement, personalized, curiosity-driven, etc.
- **Opening approach** -- Question, compliment, pain point, statistic, mutual connection, etc.
- **Value proposition** -- ROI-focused, time-saving, competitive advantage, social proof, etc.
- **Social proof type** -- Case study, metric/number, testimonial, brand name drop, etc.
- **CTA type** -- Meeting request, soft ask, question, resource offer, etc.
- **Tone** -- Formal, casual, conversational, authoritative, etc.
- **Email structure** -- Short/punchy, storytelling, problem-solution, etc.

Each dimension has enumerated values (e.g., "formal" vs. "casual" for tone) and some support numeric scoring (e.g., CTA urgency on a 1-10 scale).

### Step 2: Tags Are Correlated with Performance
Every tagged sequence is automatically linked to its campaign performance data. Outfound calculates metrics per tag value:

- Sent count, reply count, positive reply count
- Bounce rate
- Attribution metrics (sign-ups, meetings, revenue)
- Reply rate and positive reply rate

### Step 3: You See What Works
Filter by any dimension and instantly see which approaches perform best. Sort by reply rate, positive reply rate, or attributed conversions. Compare across time ranges.

## Why This Changes Everything

### Beyond Simple A/B Testing
Traditional A/B testing lets you compare Version A vs. Version B. But it doesn't tell you *what about* Version A made it work. Was it the subject line? The CTA? The tone?

Outfound's structured tagging breaks every email into its component parts and tests them independently. You might discover that:

- Questions in subject lines get 2x more opens
- Casual tone drives 40% more positive replies
- ROI-focused value props work for enterprise but not SMB
- Soft CTA ("worth a conversation?") outperforms hard CTA ("book a call")

This is **multi-dimensional copy optimization**, not binary A/B testing.

### Automatic, Not Manual
Nobody has time to manually tag hundreds of email variants across 7 dimensions. Outfound does it automatically using AI. Tags are applied the moment sequences are created, and they update as you iterate on copy.

### Works Across Campaigns
The real power emerges across campaigns. Instead of testing within a single campaign, Outfound aggregates tag performance across your entire portfolio. You're not learning from 200 sends to one audience -- you're learning from tens of thousands of sends across every audience.

**Why it's a game-changer:** For the first time, outbound teams can build a systematic, data-driven understanding of what copy elements work best for their specific market. This isn't guesswork or "best practices from a blog post." This is your data, your audience, your performance -- analyzed at a structural level.

## Tagging System Details

### Tag Dimensions
Each dimension represents one structural element of email copy. Dimensions can be:
- **Enum** -- Discrete categories (e.g., tone: formal, casual, conversational)
- **Numeric** -- Scored values with ranges (e.g., urgency: 1-10)
- **Boolean** -- Yes/no flags (e.g., includes social proof: true/false)

### Tag Sources
- **AI-generated** -- Automatically applied by Outfound's classification engine
- **User-created** -- Manually tagged by your team for custom dimensions
- **System** -- Default dimensions that come with the platform

### Custom Dimensions
You can create your own tag dimensions beyond the defaults. Track industry-specific elements, campaign types, offer categories, or any custom classification that matters to your business.

For any tag dimension, you can view:

Sort by any column. Filter by date range. Drill into specific tag values to see which campaigns use them.
